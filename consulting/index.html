Consulting & Systems Work

I work with organizations to design, build, and improve systems where reliability, accountability, and outcomes matter. My consulting centers on translating complex operational, clinical, and data challenges into systems that leaders can actually use to make decisions.

Most of my work sits at the intersection of healthcare operations, analytics, and governance, where technical choices and organizational dynamics are tightly coupled.

Focus Areas

  • Healthcare operations, quality improvement, and patient safety initiatives
  • Design and implementation of analytics and business intelligence systems
  • Decision support for clinical, operational, and financial leaders
  • Process and systems redesign informed by real-world workflow
  • Evaluation and integration of technology into existing organizational systems

How I Work

I focus on understanding the system as it exists today, including data quality, workflows, incentives, and constraints. Rather than prescribing generic solutions, I work collaboratively to design and build systems that fit the organization’s context and capacity.

This often includes hands-on work with data, reporting tools, and system design, alongside facilitation with stakeholders to align goals, expectations, and accountability.

Background

My approach is shaped by experience in nursing, infection prevention, quality improvement, informatics, and leadership roles within healthcare systems. This allows me to operate comfortably across technical, clinical, and organizational domains.

Tools & Methods

My work is grounded in systems thinking and executed through practical tools, data, and processes chosen to fit organizational context and long-term sustainability. I focus on building systems that are understandable, maintainable, and resilient rather than prescribing a fixed stack or methodology.
